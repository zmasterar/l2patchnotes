2. Grand Olympiad Enhancements

Grand Olympiad arenas have been changed to instanced zones. Matches can now be held in 160 stadiums at the same time.

Participant and Audience Limits The minimum number of participants for a match changed. Additionally, now all characters that have requested a match can enter the stadium.

Match Type Current Participant Limit Previous Participant Limit
Class-irrelevant Individual 11 characters 9 characters
Class Individual 11 characters 5 characters
Class-irrelevant Team 6 teams 9 teams

The maximum number of people who can watch a match at the same time increased to 18 per stadium.

The waiting period between matches decreased to 25 seconds (was 45 seconds).

You now cannot request a match if your character's inventory or weight is above 80%.

Camera and Replay Viewing

With the new Camera Mode, characters can move about freely while watching Grand Olympiad matches. For more information about this new mode, press the Help button while viewing a match.

A Replay function has been added to allow spectators to record a match and play it back later.

Participant Matching

The previous system in which opponents were selected randomly has been changed to a system in which opponents with similar Olympiad points are matched first. Points are now accumulated by:

  • 10 points for reaching Noblesse for the first time.
  • 10 more points every week.
  • A total of 50 points given to every character during each Olympiad cycle.

Noblesse Characters

Noblesse characters can now join only up to a set number of matches per week. This maximum number resets at the beginning of each Olympiad cycle.

  • Total Matches: 70 maximum
  • Class-Irrelevant Individual Matches: 60 maximum
  • Class Individual Matches: 30 maximum
  • Class-Irrelevant Team Matches: 10 maximum

Noblesse characters can us the /olympiadstat command on other characters to check a targeted character's Olympiad points, personal record, and remaining number of matches.

New Stadiums and Stadium Changes

The appearance of the Olympiad stadium has changed into a rectangular grassy arena surrounded by a coliseum-like building. The sky in this arena changes randomly.

5. Nevit's Advent Blessing

The swelling number of Shilen's followers and the immeasurable power of their prayers gradually led to the day of Shilen's resurrection. The angel Nevit calmly watched these events unfold, and one day, he shot the Arrow of Light down from the heavens. The Arrow held within it a vision of himself that served to bolster religious devotion to Einhasad, the nemesis of Shilen. Those affected by vision of Nevit became inspired by the power of the light.

The Hunting Bonus and its resulting Nevit's Advent Blessing are a new Vitality boost system that is a part of the Nevit's Blessings system, which also includes the Recommendation system. It automatically affects all characters logged in. This system gives a character Vitality-related boosts in direct relation to experience point gain.

Details on your character's current Hunting Bonus can be found in the game interface in the Nevit's Blessings window. This same window also houses details for the Recommendation system.

The bar below the words "Hunting Bonus" is the Hunting Bonus gauge. The gauge fills as a character gains experience points (or due to special circumstances). Once the gauge reaches 100%, your character receives Nevit's Advent Blessing. When Nevit's Advent Blessing activates, a sound effect, visual effects, and messages both on the screen and in the system messages of your chat window indicate it. A red light glows around your character and the wings icon blinks next to your full Hunting Bonus gauge.

The effect of Nevit's Advent Blessing lasts 3 minutes, after which time the red glow disappears and the Hunting Bonus gauge is reset.

The Nevit's Advent Blessing gives the following Vitality boosts all at once:

  • Vitality Recovery: Regardless of your character's current Vitality level, the character receives an experience point bonus equivalent to Vitality level 4. This effect does not stack with other Vitality recovery items.
  • Vitality Replenishing: Your character gains Vitality as it gains experience points while hunting (similar to if your character were under the effect of a Vitality Replenishing Herb). This effect does not stack with other Vitality replenishing buffs or items.
  • Negated Death Penalty: No experience points are lost upon death.

The Hunting Bonus gauge is filled by:

  • Gaining experience points from hunting. Experience points gained from quests do not apply.
  • Leveling up. Each time your character levels up, the Hunting Bonus gauge gets a big boost. The amount of this boost is the same each time. It is not dependent on level.
  • Being logged in while the Hunting Bonus timer is activated. Similar to the Recommendation system timer, the Hunting Bonus timer is activated as soon as your character kills a monster and receives experience points. As long as the Hunting Bonus timer is active, your character slowly accumulates points while they are logged in. The Hunting Bonus timer lasts 4 hours and can only be activated once per day. Every day at 6:30 a.m. (server time), your character's use of the Hunting Bonus timer is reset, and you can activate the timer once again. When the Hunting Bonus timer is active, the gradual and automatic increase to the Hunting Bonus gauge continues. The slow and automatic increase of the gauge thanks to the Hunting Bonus timer continues even when a character is dead.
  • Being randomly visited by the angel Nevit. While hunting, the angel Nevit can randomly bless your character. Nevit will give your character a visible boost to its Hunting Bonus gauge. The arrival of the angel Nevit is announced in the system messages.

The Hunting Bonus and Nevit's Advent Blessing apply to both normal and chaotic characters. Chaotic characters with 6 or more PK points are given an item drop penalty.

The Hunting Bonus gauge applies to each respective character. Its state is preserved across Subclass Changes or Subclass Separations.

The Hunting Bonus gauge does not activate or grow from gaining experience points from monsters that are nine levels or more below your character or from gaining experience points while inside a Peace Zone.

If active, the Hunting Bonus timer pauses when your character enters a Peace Zone or becomes disconnected from the server.

Nevit's Herald

When Antharas or Valakas are defeated, Nevit's Herald appears in all towns and remains for 3 hours.

Nevit's Herald can grant your character the Fall of the Dragon buff, which freezes your Recommendation and Hunting Bonus timers for 3 hours, letting you enjoy these bonuses longer.
